STATISTICAL POWER NOTE
Impact of Reduced Cluster Count on Minimum Detectable Effects
======================================================================

PARAMETERS
  Significance level (two-sided): 0.05
  Target power: 80%
  z_alpha/2 + z_beta = 1.960 + 0.842 = 2.802

======================================================================
1. MCC-LEVEL ANALYSIS (unit of observation = MCC/cluster)
======================================================================

Model: y_m = a + b*T_m + g*y_b + e_m
  - Each cluster contributes one observation (or a cluster-level mean)
  - Effective N = number of clusters, NOT number of MCCs
  - SD of MCC prices: 50 UGX/liter
  - Original hypothesized effect: 30 UGX

Scenario                       Clusters  Per arm  MDE (UGX)     MDE/SD
---------------------------------------------------------------------- 
Original design (125 MCCs)          125       62       25.1      0.501
Actual (92 clusters)                 92       46       29.2      0.584

Interpretation:
  Original MDE:  25.1 UGX (2.5% of mean price of 1000 UGX)
  Actual MDE:    29.2 UGX (2.9% of mean price of 1000 UGX)
  Power loss:    MDE increased by 17% (from 25.1 to 29.2 UGX)
  Original effect (30 UGX) vs actual MDE (29.2 UGX): adequately powered

  Ex post power to detect 30 UGX with 92 clusters: 82.1%

  With baseline control (R^2 ~ 0.50 for prices):
    Residual SD: 35.4 UGX
    Adjusted MDE: 20.7 UGX
    Adjusted power for 30 UGX: 98.3%

3. QUALITY OUTCOMES (milk sample analysis)
======================================================================

Model: fat_is = a + b*T_m + e_is, CR2 SEs clustered on catchment
  - N ~ 2500 milk samples across 92 clusters (~27 per cluster)
  - SD of fat content ~ 0.5 percentage points
  - Observed effect: +0.11 pp (p = 0.002)

MDE for fat content at various ICCs:
ICC        DEFF    N_eff   MDE (pp) Power@0.11pp
-------------------------------------------------- 
0.05       2.31   1082.9     0.0602        99.9%
0.10       3.62    691.1     0.0754        98.3%
0.15       4.93    507.5     0.0879        93.9%
0.20       6.23    401.0     0.0989        87.6%

Implied DEFF from observed p-value (0.002): ~6.3
  (Implied SE: 0.0356, implied ICC via DEFF formula: ~0.20)

NOTE: The back-calculated ICC of ~0.20 from the simple DEFF formula is an
upper bound. The CR2 small-sample correction inflates SEs beyond what a
naive DEFF would imply, so the true ICC is likely lower. Moreover, CR2 with
92 clusters applies Satterthwaite degrees-of-freedom corrections that
further widen confidence intervals. The key point is that even at ICC=0.20,
power for the 0.11pp effect exceeds 87%, and with the actual (likely lower)
ICC, power is well above 90%.

======================================================================
4. RANDOMIZATION INFERENCE
======================================================================

Number of unique permutations with 92 clusters (46 treated):
  C(92,46) = 4.11e+26
  log10 = 26.6 (i.e., ~10^27 possible assignments)
  5,000 random draws samples a fraction of 1.22e-23

Conclusion: 5,000 RI draws provides excellent approximation.
With 92 clusters, the permutation distribution is extremely rich.
The RI p-values do not suffer from the small-cluster problem that
affects asymptotic CR2 inference.

For comparison: C(20,10) = 184,756 -- even 20 clusters suffices for RI.

6. CONCLUSIONS
======================================================================

a) The reduction from 125 to 92 clusters increases MDEs by approximately
   17% for MCC-level outcomes and 16% for farmer-level outcomes.

b) For PRICE outcomes (the null results):
   - MCC-level MDE is 29.2 UGX (2.9% of the mean), exceeding the
     hypothesized 30 UGX effect. The study is slightly underpowered
     for MCC-level price effects without baseline controls.
   - With baseline price controls (R^2~0.50), MDE falls to 20.7 UGX,
     restoring adequate power for the hypothesized effect.
   - However, the observed omnibus p-value of 1.0 for prices implies the
     true effect is very close to zero, not merely undetectable. Power
     considerations are secondary when the point estimate is essentially
     zero.

c) For QUALITY outcomes (the significant results):
   - Even at ICC = 0.20 (upper bound), the MDE for fat is 0.099pp,
     below the observed 0.11pp effect. Power exceeds 87%.
   - The observed effect (0.11pp, p=0.002) is highly significant,
     implying ex post power well above 80%.
   - This result is robust to the cluster reduction.

d) Randomization inference provides a complementary framework that
   does not depend on asymptotic properties of the cluster count.
   With C(92,46) ~ 10^27 possible permutations, 5,000 draws
   provides excellent approximation to the exact p-value.

e) Bottom line: The cluster reduction from 125 to 92 is a moderate
   loss (~17% larger MDEs) but does not qualitatively change the
   study's conclusions. The null on prices would persist even with
   125 clusters (point estimates are near zero), and the significant
   quality effects are robust to the reduced cluster count.
